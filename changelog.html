April 24, 2020

The first version of the Pronoun Testing Grounds. Includes basic pronouns and six random stories. The pronoun input helpful placeholder text (with example pronouns put in a random order) and (on desktop) hover messages. There is also a couple of footnotes explaining a couple of the weird pronoun options. Some of the example pronouns are chosen randomly from a list, such as himself/hisself, and reflexive they/them pronouns.

Written for 43 North's Code:Buffalo Hackathon!

See this project on GitHub: https://github.com/mrw1593/botahamec

Visit me on Github: https://github.com/botahamec

* You may be wondering what the difference between the possesseive determiner and the possessive pronoun is. That's a good question. There doesn't seem to be one. I think it's a remnant of Old English. I wish it didn't exist because it's much harder to come up with stories with it in there. General rule of thumb: The possessive determiner comes before the verb, and the possessive pronoun comes after the verb.

** The plural is mostly used for people who use they/them pronouns. Some people like to use singular verbs with these pronouns. For example, "They is happy" vs "They are happy". Another example: "They wakes up in the morning" vs "They wake up in the morning". If you're confused about which one to use, then unless you're using they/them pronouns, you're probably gonna want "singular".
